## The problem with most AI pitches

Most vendor decks still lead with model size, benchmarks and “transformational” claims. CTOs don’t buy that. They buy **predictable outcomes, safe deployments, and a team that can ship in the constraints of real systems**. In 2026, the gap between AI demos and production reality is still where budgets go to die.

This checklist reflects what CTOs actually ask for in buyer conversations — and what separates vendors who *sell hype* from vendors who *deliver value*.

---

## The 2026 CTO Buyer Checklist

### 1) A clear business outcome (not just capability)
- **What will change in the business** in 90 days?
- **Which KPI moves** and how is it measured?
- **What gets removed from the process** (people time, manual steps, rework)?

**Signal to look for:** A vendor that can tell you *what stops being a problem* — in your context — not just what the model can do.

### 2) Integration with your existing stack
- Can it **plug into your existing workflows** (Jira, ServiceNow, Slack/Teams, CRM, ERP)?
- Does it respect **your data boundaries** and permissions?
- How does it handle **document sprawl** (SharePoint, Google Drive, local drives)?

**Signal to look for:** A vendor who shows you the integration map first, not the demo.

### 3) Data handling and governance you can explain to a regulator
- Where is data stored? For how long?
- Can you **bring your own model** or use a private runtime?
- Is there **auditability** of decisions and outputs?

**Signal to look for:** Clear, simple governance answers — without legal gymnastics.

### 4) Reliability, fallback and failure modes
- What happens when the model is wrong?
- Is there a **human approval loop**?
- What’s the **rollback plan** if adoption stalls?

**Signal to look for:** A vendor who treats errors as normal and designs for recovery.

### 5) Adoption economics (not just licence price)
- What’s the **training and change cost**?
- How much time does it take a frontline team to feel confident?
- What’s the **total cost per outcome**, not per seat?

**Signal to look for:** Vendors who talk about **time saved**, **error reduction**, and **risk reduction** — not just cost per user.

### 6) Vendor posture: are they your partner or your experiment?
- Who owns the roadmap if your needs shift?
- Do they provide **real implementation support**, not just tech?
- Can they show **production references** in your sector?

**Signal to look for:** A vendor who is transparent about what *isn’t* ready yet.

### 7) Security posture that fits your reality
- SSO, RBAC, SOC2/ISO alignment (minimum bar)
- Data isolation and encryption at rest/in transit
- Incident response and reporting cadence

**Signal to look for:** A vendor that can slot into your existing security review without a three‑month detour.

## Red flags we keep seeing

- **“We can customise anything.”** Translation: they haven’t built anything repeatable yet.
- **“Just plug it in.”** Integration is never “just.” Ask for their last three implementation timelines.
- **“We’re benchmark‑leading.”** Benchmarks don’t run your business.
- **“Trust us, it’s safe.”** No audit trail = no production approval.

---

## The questions CTOs should ask (verbatim)

1. *Show me the workflow before and after. Where is human oversight?*
2. *What happens when the model is wrong — who gets notified, and how do we recover?*
3. *What is the minimum integration footprint to get value in 30–60 days?*
4. *What’s your plan for data residency and auditability?*
5. *How do you prove ROI in a way my CFO will accept?*

---

## The bottom line

In 2026, AI procurement is less about **which model is best** and more about **which vendor can deliver safe, measurable outcomes inside your constraints**. CTOs don’t want a shiny demo; they want **predictable delivery**.

If a vendor can’t explain outcomes, governance, integration and adoption in plain language — they’re not ready for production.
